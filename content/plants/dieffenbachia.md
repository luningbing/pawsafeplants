---
title: Dieffenbachia
scientific_name: Dieffenbachia seguine
toxicity_level: ⚠️ MILD TO MODERATE TOXICITY – ORAL AND GASTROINTESTINAL IRRITATION
summary: >-
  Dieffenbachia contains calcium oxalate crystals which can cause severe oral
  irritation and swelling in cats.
image: 'https://picsum.photos/800/600?random=5'
care_difficulty: Medium
common_names:
  - Dieffenbachia
  - Dumb Cane
is_flower: false
ascpa_link: >-
  https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/dieffenbachia
symptoms:
  - Oral irritation
  - Swelling
  - Difficulty swallowing
  - Excessive drooling
what_to_do: >-
  **If your cat ingests dieffenbachia:**


  1. Remove any remaining plant material from your cat's mouth.

  2. Rinse the mouth thoroughly with cool water.

  3. Offer a small amount of water or milk to help flush the system.

  4. Monitor for symptoms and contact your veterinarian if they persist or
  worsen.

  5. Do not induce vomiting unless specifically instructed by your vet.


  **Prevention:**

  - Keep Dieffenbachia out of reach of curious cats

  - Consider placing in hanging baskets or high shelves

  - Provide cat-safe alternatives like cat grass or catnip
safe_alternatives:
  - Prayer Plant
  - Peperomia
