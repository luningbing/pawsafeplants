---
title: Tomato Plant
scientific_name: Solanum lycopersicum
toxicity_level: ⚠️ MILD TO MODERATE TOXICITY – ORAL AND GASTROINTESTINAL IRRITATION
summary: >-
  Tomato plants contain solanine and tomatine which can cause gastrointestinal
  upset in cats.
image: >-
  https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1501004318641-b398d68e866b?ixlib=rb-4.0.3&w=800&h=600&fit=crop
care_difficulty: Medium
common_names:
  - Tomato Plant
  - Tomato
is_flower: false
ascpa_link: >-
  https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/tomato
symptoms:
  - Vomiting
  - Diarrhea
  - Lethargy
  - Weakness
what_to_do: >-
  **If your cat ingests tomato plant:**


  1. Remove any remaining plant material from your cat's mouth.

  2. Rinse the mouth thoroughly with cool water.

  3. Offer a small amount of water or milk to help flush the system.

  4. Monitor for symptoms and contact your veterinarian if they persist or
  worsen.

  5. Do not induce vomiting unless specifically instructed by your vet.


  **Prevention:**

  - Keep Tomato Plant out of reach of curious cats

  - Consider placing in hanging baskets or high shelves

  - Provide cat-safe alternatives like cat grass or catnip
safe_alternatives:
  - Basil
  - Catnip
