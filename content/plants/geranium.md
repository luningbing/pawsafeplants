---
title: Geranium
scientific_name: Pelargonium x hortorum
common_names:
  - Garden Geranium
  - Zonal Geranium
toxicity_level: ⚠️ MILD TO MODERATE TOXICITY – GASTROINTESTINAL IRRITATION
summary: >-
  Geraniums contain geraniol and other compounds that can cause vomiting,
  diarrhea, and lethargy in cats. Ingestion should be avoided, though severe
  outcomes are rare.
ascpa_link: >-
  https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/geranium
image: /images/plants/geranium.svg
symptoms:
  - Vomiting
  - Diarrhea
  - Lethargy
  - Skin irritation (from prolonged handling)
what_to_do: |-
  **If your cat consumes geranium, contact your vet.**

  1. Rinse the mouth gently with water.
  2. Offer a small amount of milk or water to dilute irritants.
  3. Keep your cat hydrated and monitor for worsening symptoms.
  4. Seek veterinary care if vomiting or diarrhea continues.
safe_alternatives:
  - African Violet (non-toxic flowering plant)
  - >-
    Begonia (note: begonia also has mild toxicity; choose non-toxic
    alternatives)
  - Impatiens (non-toxic)
sources:
  - ASPCA Toxic and Non-Toxic Plants Database
  - 'Veterinary Partner: Geranium Toxicity'
---
