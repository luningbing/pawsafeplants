---
title: Cyclamen
scientific_name: Cyclamen persicum
toxicity_level: Danger
summary: >-
  Cyclamen contains cyclamine which can cause severe vomiting and diarrhea in
  cats.
image: 'https://picsum.photos/800/600?random=10'
care_difficulty: Medium
common_names:
  - Cyclamen
  - Persian Violet
is_flower: true
ascpa_link: >-
  https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/cyclamen
symptoms:
  - Severe vomiting
  - Diarrhea
  - Heart problems
  - Death
what_to_do: >-
  **If your cat ingests cyclamen:**


  1. Remove any remaining plant material from your cat's mouth.

  2. Rinse the mouth thoroughly with cool water.

  3. Offer a small amount of water or milk to help flush the system.

  4. Monitor for symptoms and contact your veterinarian if they persist or
  worsen.

  5. Do not induce vomiting unless specifically instructed by your vet.


  **Prevention:**

  - Keep Cyclamen out of reach of curious cats

  - Consider placing in hanging baskets or high shelves

  - Provide cat-safe alternatives like cat grass or catnip
safe_alternatives:
  - African Violet
  - Primrose
