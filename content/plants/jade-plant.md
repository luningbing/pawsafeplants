---
title: Jade Plant
scientific_name: Crassula ovata
common_names:
  - Money Plant
  - Friendship Tree
toxicity_level: ⚠️ MILD TO MODERATE TOXICITY – GASTROINTESTINAL DISTRESS
summary: >-
  Jade plants contain compounds that can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and lethargy
  in cats if ingested. While not usually life-threatening, prompt veterinary
  attention is recommended.
ascpa_link: >-
  https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/jade-plant
image: 'https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1543168256-739e5d9d537a?w=800&h=600&fit=crop'
symptoms:
  - Vomiting
  - Diarrhea
  - Lethargy
  - Incoordination
  - Depression
what_to_do: |-
  **Contact your veterinarian if your cat eats jade plant.**

  1. Remove any plant material from the mouth.
  2. Offer water to dilute.
  3. Monitor for symptoms; seek veterinary care if vomiting persists.
  4. Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by a professional.
safe_alternatives:
  - 'Haworthia (similar succulent, non-toxic)'
  - Echeveria (non-toxic succulent)
  - Burro's Tail (non-toxic succulent)
sources:
  - ASPCA Toxic and Non-Toxic Plants Database
  - 'PetMD: Jade Plant Toxicity in Cats'
---
