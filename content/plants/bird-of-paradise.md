---
title: Bird of Paradise
scientific_name: Strelitzia reginae
toxicity_level: Danger
summary: >-
  Bird of Paradise contains hydrocyanic acid which can cause gastrointestinal
  irritation and vomiting in cats.
image: 'https://picsum.photos/800/600?random=8'
care_difficulty: Medium
common_names:
  - Bird of Paradise
  - Crane Flower
is_flower: false
ascpa_link: >-
  https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants/bird-of-paradise
symptoms:
  - Vomiting
  - Drowsiness
  - Loss of appetite
what_to_do: >-
  **If your cat ingests bird of paradise:**


  1. Remove any remaining plant material from your cat's mouth.

  2. Rinse the mouth thoroughly with cool water.

  3. Offer a small amount of water or milk to help flush the system.

  4. Monitor for symptoms and contact your veterinarian if they persist or
  worsen.

  5. Do not induce vomiting unless specifically instructed by your vet.


  **Prevention:**

  - Keep Bird of Paradise out of reach of curious cats

  - Consider placing in hanging baskets or high shelves

  - Provide cat-safe alternatives like cat grass or catnip
safe_alternatives:
  - Orchids
  - African Violet
