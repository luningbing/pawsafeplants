Beaucarnea recurvata, despite its name, is not a true palm but a member of the Asparagaceae family. Native to Mexico, it stores water in its thick trunk, allowing it to withstand drought. The plant thrives in bright light and infrequent watering. Its whimsical, hair-like leaves give it a distinctive appearance that becomes more dramatic with age.
