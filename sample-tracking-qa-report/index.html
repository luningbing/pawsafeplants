1. Final ad landing page URL

This is the URL a beginner seller can paste into the ad platform after checking that the page opens correctly.

https://example.com/products/sample-product?utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid_social&utm_campaign=spring_sale_travel_bag&utm_content=hero_video

2. Parameter map

Parameter Sample value Why it exists
utm_source meta Groups traffic from Facebook and Instagram ads.
utm_medium paid_social Keeps paid social separate from organic posts, email, or affiliates.
utm_campaign spring_sale_travel_bag Readable campaign name for GA4 and team discussion.
utm_content hero_video Simple creative label for comparing ad concepts.

3. Ad setup brief

This is the copyable handoff a small team can paste into a campaign task or send to the person setting up ads.

Ad Landing Page Setup Brief

Final landing page URL:
https://example.com/products/sample-product?utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid_social&utm_campaign=spring_sale_travel_bag&utm_content=hero_video

Copy these URL fields into the ad platform or campaign brief:
- utm_source: meta
- utm_medium: paid_social
- utm_campaign: spring_sale_travel_bag
- utm_content: hero_video

Before spending:
1. Open GA4 Realtime.
2. Click the final landing page URL.
3. Confirm the product page opens correctly.
4. Confirm GA4 shows the visit.
5. Confirm source, medium, campaign, and content look right.

4. GA4 Realtime QA steps

  1. Open GA4 Realtime before clicking the final landing page URL.
  2. Click the URL from a normal browser tab, not only from the site builder.
  3. Confirm the store page opens without an error or unexpected redirect.
  4. Confirm GA4 shows an active user and a page_view.
  5. Confirm campaign fields are readable enough for the seller to recognize the ad.
  6. If ecommerce tracking is installed, check view_item, add_to_cart, begin_checkout, and purchase where supported.

5. What this sample does not promise

  • It does not promise perfect attribution. Browser privacy, consent settings, redirects, checkout behavior, cross-domain flows, and ad blockers can affect analytics.
  • It does not hide the destination or bypass ad platform rules.
  • It does not put customer emails, phone numbers, names, addresses, or order notes in URL parameters.
  • It does not replace the ad platform, ecommerce platform, payment provider, or GA4 reporting limits.
